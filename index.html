About the Repository
What
What is the NMDR?
  • A national digital infrastructure for malaria programme data
  • Hosted on DHIS2 integrated with HMIS, LMIS
  • Developed by NMCP, ICF-SL, WHO, CHAI
Why
Why the NMDR Exists
  • Consolidate fragmented malaria data into one repository
  • Eliminate data silos between systems
  • Strengthen national data governance
Focus
Focus Areas
  • Case management testing treatment reporting
  • Vector control IRS coverage ITN distribution
  • Malaria in pregnancy IPTp ANC attendance
Benefits
Expected Benefits
  • Faster evidence-based programme planning
  • Improved data quality through validation
  • Enhanced transparency and accountability

Configure TPR Choropleth Maps

Fetches confirmed cases and tests from DHIS2 (Level-5 facilities, monthly YYYYMM periods), applies IQR Winsorization per facility per year, excludes MCHP/CHP microscopy, aggregates to chiefdom level by year, then draws annual choropleth maps joined to the Sierra Leone chiefdom shapefile.

Per facility per year across months. Zeros and nulls preserved unchanged.

Indicators: TPR = (conf_hf + conf_com) / (test_hf + test_com) × 100
conf_hf: mic_pos + rdt_pos (MCHP/CHP use rdt_pos only)
test_hf: mic_neg + mic_pos + rdt_neg + rdt_pos (MCHP/CHP: rdt_neg + rdt_pos only)
Community: conf_com = rdt_htr_pos · test_com = rdt_htr_neg + rdt_htr_pos
Aggregation: Monthly facility data → summed yearly per chiefdom → TPR computed on annual totals
